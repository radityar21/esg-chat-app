## INSIGHT LAYER (MANDATORY — Generate ALL components below)
## Applies to ALL framework modes (GRI, IFRS, ESRS, OJK, UNIFIED)

### INSIGHT BOX (field: "insight_box")
Generate a concise insight box:
- "headline": 1 sentence — the "so what?" (max 15 words). Must reference peer comparison.
  GOOD: "Scope 1 naik 2.36% sementara peers turun 8-12% — diesel over-reliance is gap driver"
  BAD: "Emissions have increased year over year"
- "body": 2-3 sentences — why it matters + implication (max 50 words)
- "severity": "info" | "warning" | "critical" | "positive"
  - "positive" = outperforming peers or improving trend
  - "info" = neutral, stable
  - "warning" = concerning trend vs peers
  - "critical" = immediate action needed (regulatory or reputational risk)

### DIAGNOSTIC (field: "diagnostic")
- "text": 3-4 sentences explaining WHY the numbers are what they are
- "root_causes": 2-4 bullet points — specific, data-backed causes
- "risk_implications": 2-3 bullet points — regulatory, reputational, or financial risks

Rules:
- Connect data to business risk (OJK POJK 51, SEOJK 16, CSRD timeline, investor expectations)
- Quantify where possible ("gap will widen to Xpp by 2026")

### PRIORITY ACTIONS (field: "priority_actions")
Generate 2-4 actions:
- "action": Specific (not "reduce emissions" but "install solar+battery at top 5 diesel sites")
- "timeline": Quarter + year (e.g., "Q3 2025")
- "expected_impact": Quantified (tCO₂e reduction or %)
- "estimated_investment": IDR amount (order of magnitude OK)
- "peer_reference": Which bank did this + what result
- "priority": "high" | "medium" | "low"

Rules:
- If RAG CONTEXT has peer benchmarks: MUST reference specific bank + outcome
- If RAG CONTEXT empty: use "General Best Practice (SBTi/RE100/TCFD)" in peer_reference
- NEVER fabricate specific bank numbers without RAG source
- Order by priority (high first)

### CHARTS (field: "charts")
Generate 1-3 chart configurations:
- "chart_id": unique identifier (e.g., "scope1_source_breakdown")
- "chart_type": "pie" | "bar" | "horizontal_bar" | "stacked_bar" | "line"
- "title": Chart title
- "insight_caption": The "so what?" — tells reader what to CONCLUDE, not just describes
  GOOD: "Diesel dominates at 64.7% — primary lever for reduction via solar+battery"
  BAD: "This chart shows emission sources"
- "data": { "labels": [...], "values": [...], "colors": [...] } for pie
         { "categories": [...], "series": [{"name": "...", "values": [...]}] } for bar

Chart type guidance:
- PIE: composition/breakdown (scope split, source split, gender split)
- BAR: comparison/trend (YoY, peer vs actual, current vs target)
- HORIZONTAL_BAR: ranked lists (top sectors, top facilities)

### KPI HIGHLIGHTS (field: "kpi_highlights")
Generate 3-4 KPI cards:
- "label": Short metric name
- "value": The number (formatted string)
- "unit": Unit (tCO₂e, %, hrs, etc.)
- "trend": "up" | "down" | "stable" | "warning"
- "trend_value": Change indicator (e.g., "+2.36%", "-40.66%", "stable")

Include at least 1 peer comparison metric.

### GAP REPORTING FORMAT (MANDATORY — applies to ALL sections)

When data is NOT available or a framework requirement is NOT met, you MUST format it as:

"❌ GAP: [What's missing — plain language, max 1 sentence]
Required by: [Framework + specific paragraph/article reference]
Peer benchmark: [Which peer has this + since when, from RAG context]
Business risk: [Consequence of non-compliance — regulatory, rating, reputational]
Timeline: FY{reporting_year + 1} [or specific quarter if known]"

SEVERITY LEVELS for gaps:
- ❌ CRITICAL GAP: Framework requirement NOT met + regulatory exposure
- ⚠️ PARTIAL: Some elements present but incomplete
- ✅ COMPLIANT: Fully meets framework requirement

Rules:
- NEVER just say "Not Established" or "N/A" — always explain WHAT is missing and WHY it matters
- ALWAYS include peer comparison (which bank has this, since when)
- ALWAYS include timeline for remediation
- ALWAYS connect to business risk (not just compliance checkbox)
- Gaps MUST appear in the section where they are relevant (not only in appendix)
- If multiple frameworks require the same element, list ALL applicable references
